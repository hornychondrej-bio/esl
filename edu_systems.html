Where Would You Like to Go to School?

A 2026 comparative analysis of education systems in the United States, Great Britain, and the Czech Republic.

The Educational Journey

While children in all three countries begin their compulsory education around age five or six, the structure and duration of primary and secondary schooling vary significantly. The chart below illustrates the typical duration in years for each phase of schooling leading up to higher education.

Duration of Compulsory & Secondary Education

Comparing the years spent in primary vs. secondary/high school environments before university.

🇺🇸 United States

Follows a typical 5-3-4 structure: 5 years elementary, 3 years middle school, and 4 years high school (freshmen to seniors).

🇬🇧 Great Britain

Divided into primary (infant/junior) up to age 11, followed by secondary school and optional Sixth Form (Years 12-13).

🇨🇿 Czech Republic

Features a distinct 9-year unified elementary school (*základní škola*), followed by 4 years of secondary/grammar school.

Assessing Success: Exams & Grading

How is a student's knowledge measured? From daily grading systems to the final high-stakes exams that determine university placement, each nation has its own approach to academic assessment.

💻

🇺🇸 United States

Grading System
A to F (No 'E' grade)
Final Assessments

Digital SAT & ACT

Taken in junior year. As of 2026, the SAT is a fully digital, adaptive test taken on laptops/tablets. High school testing relies heavily on quizzes and projects rather than oral exams.

📝

🇬🇧 Great Britain

Grading System
A* to U (U = Unclassified/Fail)
Final Assessments

GCSEs & A-Levels

GCSEs taken at 16. A-Levels (3-4 subjects) taken at 18. AS-Levels are now decoupled. Vocational options include the new T-Levels.

🗣️

🇨🇿 Czech Republic

Grading System
1 to 5 (1 is Excellent)
Final Assessments

Maturita

Final school-leaving exam in the 4th year of secondary school. As of 2025/2026, includes the "Complex Profile Work" (a project/thesis) as a modern alternative. Oral exams are common.
